POS and In-store Retail devices

Today there is a growing use of android mobile devices – vending machines, kiosks, advertising to name a few apart from the classic mobile use. Point-of-sale devices in particular have had a significant increase in recent years. These devices streamline the sale at the end customer, but managing devices located on multiple sites can be a challenge. Providing remote support to a number devices from a central IT team is a solution that allows not only instant real-time support, but saves money and time to both the end-user and to the team managing these.

Another reason why remote management of POS android devices has to be considered is the end user: it can not be expected that the user holding the actual device will be technologically savvy enough to be able to navigate on the device on their own, however making an IT technician go to the location all the time is not feasible. Remote access management seems to be the answer to both of those challenges.

An additional advantage to use remote access management to POS android devices can be if these devices can be grouped and managed accordingly. Rolling out a new app to these groups or collecting data from them can be simplified further optimizing the processes.

Compared to most of the previous technologies, many of the android POS devices are in fact mobile; this means that tracking their geographic location is a must in terms of security and a source of useful insight from the possible data that can be gathered.

At Webkey, we have a solution ready to all the above issues. We understand these challenges, as we have faced them while helping our customers. We are ready to help you as well, so do not hesitate to get in touch!

Digital Warehouse and logistics

The transportation and logistics (T&L) industry has experienced a boom during the COVID-19 pandemic. Additional to this, the mobile devices from smartphones to IoT devices have brought about a world in T&L that means fiercer competition than ever before.Â  With all the increased volumes, the technical opportunities and expansion of the industry it is key for any players involved with transportation, logistics, shipping or warehousing to stay ahead of their competitors.Â 

One way to ensure that a T&L company stays ahead of the race is to implement mobile device management (MDM solution) softwares that enables you to have better control and overview over your fleet of devices. A cleverly set-up Mobile device Management software can help secure your data, control information centrally, track them via GPS or provide immediate and central support.Â 

It would be a luxury for any T&L company to lose time or money because of devices that are down due technical errors. With Webkey, it is possible to provide support from a central station to any Android devices that are out on a mission with the drivers. Webkey helps you to overcome all sorts of issues from broken screens obscuring vital information to making sure that all other apps on the Android devices are up-to-date across all devices in your fleet.

Tracing your devices on maps is almost a must-have these days: Webkey can show a group of devices on a map – in addition to some of the obvious logistical advantages, this can further allow you to gather information from your devices when they are in a certain area or display ads on your devices when they are in certain locations.Â 

Security of operations and data are of the highest importance in the T&L industry. Webkey has a ready answer as remote control sessions are encrypted between the browser and your device by SSL/TLS certificates. The pairing of devices always requires a PIN eliminating the malicious pairings. Additionally it is possible to run Webkey on a local access network on servers owned by you, removing many possibilities of data loss on the world wide web.Â
